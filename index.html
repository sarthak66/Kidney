Chronic kidney disease

Chronic kidney disease describes the gradual loss of kidney function. Your kidneys filter wastes your blood, which are then excreted in your urine. When chronic kidney disease reaches dangerous levels of fluid, wastes can build up in your body.

Symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Muscle twitches and cramps
  • Variation in urine output
  • Headache
  • at late stage swelling of face and limbs
